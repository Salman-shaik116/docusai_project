HbA1c (Hemoglobin A1c)
Biomarker

HbA1c estimates average blood sugar over the previous two to three months. It is commonly used to screen for diabetes and monitor long-term glucose control.

Commonly reviewed in diabetes care Often discussed with fasting glucose
LDL Cholesterol
Cardiometabolic

LDL is often called "bad" cholesterol because elevated levels can contribute to plaque buildup inside arteries. It is a standard part of cardiovascular risk review.

Reviewed in lipid panels Often paired with HDL and triglycerides
CRP (C-Reactive Protein)
Inflammation

CRP is a marker associated with inflammation in the body. Depending on the context, it can help clinicians assess infection, inflammatory activity, or broader cardiovascular risk.

Used in inflammatory review Interpretation depends on clinical context
TSH (Thyroid Stimulating Hormone)
Hormone Test

TSH helps evaluate thyroid function. Abnormal results may suggest the thyroid is underactive or overactive, especially when interpreted with free T4 or free T3.

Common endocrine screening marker Often reviewed with thyroid symptoms
