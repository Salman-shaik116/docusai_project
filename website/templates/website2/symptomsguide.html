Common symptoms people start with

Each symptom can range from mild and routine to something that deserves quicker medical attention depending on severity, duration, and related signs.

HD

Headache

Often linked to tension, dehydration, infection, vision strain, or sleep disruption, but sudden severe headache patterns deserve faster review.

Low Medium
FT

Fatigue

Common with stress, illness, poor sleep, nutrition issues, and chronic conditions. Persistent fatigue may need broader clinical context.

Low Medium
CP

Chest Pain

Can relate to muscle strain, reflux, anxiety, or more serious cardiac and pulmonary causes depending on pattern and associated symptoms.

Medium High
NV

Nausea

May appear with infections, medication effects, stomach conditions, migraines, or pregnancy-related changes depending on history.

Low Medium
BP

Back Pain

Frequently mechanical, posture-related, or strain-based, but nerve symptoms, injury history, or weakness change the urgency picture.

Medium High
FV

Fever

Usually signals infection or inflammation. Duration, age, immune status, and other symptoms all affect how concerning it is.

Low Medium

Browse by body system

Symptoms become easier to interpret when grouped by the body system they most likely affect.

RS

Respiratory symptoms

Cough, congestion, breathlessness, wheezing, sore throat, and fever patterns that may connect to infection or airway issues.

CV

Cardiovascular symptoms

Chest discomfort, palpitations, dizziness, swelling, and exertional symptoms that may need closer attention.

GI

Digestive symptoms

Abdominal pain, nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, reflux, and appetite changes with varying urgency depending on severity.

When to escalate beyond a simple guide

A symptom guide helps with orientation, but some situations should move quickly into direct clinical support.

Use AI Doctor for better context

If symptoms are ongoing, recurring, or connected to other findings in your history, AI Doctor can help you organize details before your next step.

Use lab interpretation when results are involved

If symptoms come with blood work, biomarkers, or uploaded reports, the lab workflow can help turn those results into clearer follow-up questions.

Consider urgent care when red flags appear

Severe breathing difficulty, fainting, stroke-like symptoms, sudden intense pain, or rapidly worsening status should not wait for a general guide.

Request a second opinion for complex cases

When symptoms are persistent and the diagnosis or treatment plan still feels unclear, a second opinion can be the more appropriate next move.
